Instructions

In the following experiment, you are asked to provide labels for 186 image collections, each representing a range of object properties. Here is an example collection:

High Low

Labels can be descriptive words or categories. Each example shows objects ordered from high to low importance within that collection. Your task is to come up with verbal labels that describe the shared properties or categories for each of the collections. When providing labels, make sure to consider the full range of objects in the collection. You can provide at least one and up to as many verbal labels for each collection as you think are appropriate.

For the example collection above, possible labels could include:

  • vessel
  • round
  • used to cook
  • food-related

Remember, dimensions in these collections may represent multiple qualities. Aim to be as comprehensive as possible when generating your labels.

After labeling each collection, we ask you to rate its interpretability on a scale from 1 to 7, with 1 being the least interpretable and 7 being the most interpretable. Interpretability refers to how easy it is to understand the relationship between the images, particularly in the upper rows.
