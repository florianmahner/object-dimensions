Instructions

In this experiment, we present you with a series of image samples, with each sample containing a grid of 5 x 6 images:

High Low

You will be asked to come up with labels for 186 samples like this. The task consists of two steps:

  1. Understand the quality represented in the images and how it changes from high to low values.
  2. Generate up to five short labels that describe this quality.

The experiment will begin after this screen. The objects in the images are representative examples of each level along the scale you can see on the left, ranging from high to low. Even though examples in the middle part of the scale may seem less representative, they are still meaningful and should play a role in your judgment to a certain degree. In other words, do not solely rely on the commonalities of the top few images, but inspect how the object quality continuously decreases.

Your task is to describe each collection with up to 5 specific words that capture the essence of the images. Keep in mind that the dimensions in these collections can represent more than one quality.

After labeling each collection, rate its interpretability on a scale from 1 to 7, with 1 being the least interpretable and 7 being the most interpretable. Interpretability refers to the ease of understanding the relationship between the images, primarily in the upper rows.

Remember, dimensions in these collections may represent multiple qualities. Be as comprehensive as possible when generating your labels.
